## Project Overview
Healio is a user-centric mobile application concept designed to support youth mental health. Developed as part of a User Experience Engineering initiative, the project focuses on creating a safe, inclusive, and stigma-free digital environment for emotional tracking and mental well-being support.

## Problem Statement
Young people often hesitate to seek professional mental health support due to privacy concerns, social stigma, and fear of judgment. The challenge was to design an interface that feels personal, trustworthy, and accessibleâ€”bridging the gap between self-care and trusted social support.

## Design Process (Double Diamond)

### Discover & Define
- Conducted user research through surveys and interviews with students and young professionals
- Identified key pain points such as emotional isolation, lack of expression, and fear of exposure
- Created detailed **User Personas** and **Empathy Maps** to guide design decisions

### Ideate
- Facilitated brainstorming and sketching sessions
- Designed user flows for:
  - Mood logging
  - Crisis alerts
  - Trusted guardian connections

### Design & Prototype
- Progressed from low-fidelity wireframes to a high-fidelity interactive prototype in Figma
- Applied **Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion (EDI)** principles
- Ensured accessibility through colorblind-safe palettes and readable typography

## Key Features
- **Mood Logging:** Multi-modal input using emojis, voice, or text
- **Trusted Person Dashboard:** Secure view for guardians to monitor well-being without invading privacy
- **Visual Identity:** Calm, non-clinical UI to reduce anxiety and encourage daily engagement
